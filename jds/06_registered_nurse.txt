Job Title: Registered Nurse - Emergency Department

Facility: Metropolitan General Hospital

About Metropolitan General Hospital:
Metropolitan General Hospital is a 500-bed Level I Trauma Center serving the greater metropolitan area. Our Emergency Department sees over 80,000 patients annually and is known for excellence in trauma care, cardiac emergencies, and stroke treatment. We are a teaching hospital affiliated with State University Medical School.

Position Summary:
We are seeking a dedicated and skilled Registered Nurse to join our Emergency Department team. The ideal candidate will thrive in a fast-paced environment, demonstrate clinical excellence, and provide compassionate patient-centered care.

Essential Duties and Responsibilities:
- Provide direct patient care to a diverse population including trauma, medical emergencies, pediatric cases, and psychiatric emergencies
- Perform comprehensive patient assessments and develop individualized care plans
- Administer medications and treatments according to physician orders and hospital protocols
- Initiate IV access, perform phlebotomy, and manage central lines
- Monitor patients for changes in condition and respond appropriately to clinical deterioration
- Operate and troubleshoot emergency medical equipment including cardiac monitors, ventilators, and defibrillators
- Triage incoming patients using ESI (Emergency Severity Index) protocols
- Collaborate with physicians, specialists, and multidisciplinary teams
- Document patient care accurately and thoroughly in Epic EMR
- Educate patients and families about diagnoses, treatments, and discharge instructions
- Participate in emergency codes, rapid responses, and trauma activations
- Maintain compliance with infection control protocols and safety standards
- Precept and mentor new nurses and nursing students

Required Qualifications:
- Current RN license in good standing in the state
- Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN) required; ADN considered with commitment to complete BSN within 2 years
- Minimum 2 years of acute care nursing experience; ED experience strongly preferred
- Current BLS, ACLS, PALS certifications
- TNCC (Trauma Nursing Core Course) certification required within 6 months of hire
- Strong clinical assessment and critical thinking skills
- Ability to work under pressure and prioritize in high-acuity situations
- Excellent communication and interpersonal skills
- Physical ability to lift 50+ pounds, stand for extended periods, and respond quickly to emergencies

Preferred Qualifications:
- CEN (Certified Emergency Nurse) certification
- ENPC (Emergency Nursing Pediatric Course) certification
- Experience with Level I Trauma Center patient population
- Previous charge nurse or preceptor experience
- Bilingual (Spanish) a plus

Work Schedule:
- Full-time, 36 hours per week
- 12-hour shifts (7am-7pm or 7pm-7am)
- Rotating weekends and holidays required
- Must be available for on-call shifts

- Relocation assistance available
- Medical, dental, and vision insurance
- 403(b) retirement plan with employer match
- Paid time off and sick leave
- Tuition reimbursement up to $5,250/year
- Free parking and scrub allowance
- Employee wellness programs
