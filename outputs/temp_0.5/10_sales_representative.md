## **Recruiter Brief: Enterprise Sales Representative – CloudSecure Technologies**

### **1. About CloudSecure Technologies (Context for Candidates)**
- **Industry:** Cybersecurity (focuses on keeping companies’ data and systems safe from online threats)
- **What We Do:** We provide advanced software that helps large companies protect their digital assets (like data, systems, and applications) in the cloud (online storage and computing).
- **Our Customers:** Over 500 large companies (“enterprises”) worldwide, protecting more than $2 trillion in digital assets.
- **Company Size & Growth:** Fast-growing, with over $150 million in funding and doubling in size every year.
- **Location:** Headquarters in San Francisco, but this role is remote within the Western US.

---

### **2. Role Overview**
- **Job Title:** Enterprise Sales Representative
- **Goal:** Find and close new business deals with very large companies (5,000+ employees), focusing on the Western US region.
- **Type of Sale:** “Enterprise” means selling to very large companies, often with long, complicated sales cycles (6-12 months from first meeting to closing a deal).
- **What Success Looks Like:** Hitting or exceeding a sales target of $1.5M-$2M per year in new deals.

---

### **3. Key Responsibilities (What the Candidate Will Do)**
- **Find New Customers:** Identify, contact, and build relationships with large companies that could become new clients.
- **Manage Sales Pipeline:** Keep track of multiple potential deals at once (ideally 3-4 times the annual sales target in potential deals).
- **Understand Customer Needs:** Talk to customers to learn about their security challenges and needs.
- **Present Solutions:** Show customers how our products work, explain the benefits, and give presentations.
- **Lead the Sales Process:** Guide the customer through a long buying process, sometimes lasting up to a year.
- **Negotiate & Close Deals:** Lead contract negotiations and close deals ranging from $200,000 to $2 million or more in annual value.
- **Work with Executives:** Build relationships with top executives responsible for technology and security (CISO, CIO, CTO).
- **Collaborate Internally:** Work closely with:
  - Sales Engineers (who handle technical details)
  - Marketing (for campaigns targeting specific companies)
  - Business Development Reps (who help find new leads)
  - Product Team (to share customer feedback)
  - Customer Success Team (for customer references)

---

### **4. Candidate Profile (What to Look For)**

#### **Must-Have Experience**
- **Education:** Bachelor’s degree (any field).
- **Sales Experience:** At least 5-7 years selling to other businesses (not consumers).
- **Enterprise Sales:** At least 3 years selling software or technology to large companies.
- **Deal Size:** Has closed deals worth at least $500,000 per year.
- **Complex Sales:** Experience managing long, complicated sales processes involving multiple decision-makers.
- **CRM Skills:** Comfortable using sales tracking tools like Salesforce.
- **Presentation Skills:** Strong at giving presentations and communicating with executives.
- **Travel:** Willing and able to travel 30-40% of the time for meetings and events.

#### **Preferred/Bonus Experience**
- **Cybersecurity/Cloud:** Has sold cybersecurity, cloud infrastructure, or related enterprise software.
- **CISO Relationships:** Already knows or has sold to Chief Information Security Officers (CISOs) at large companies.
- **Technical Aptitude:** Can understand and explain technical concepts (doesn’t need to be an engineer).
- **Sales Methodology:** Familiar with structured sales processes like MEDDIC (a popular method for managing complex sales).
- **Startup/High-Growth:** Has worked at a fast-growing company before.

#### **Personal Qualities**
- **Hunter Mentality:** Enjoys finding and winning new business, not just managing existing accounts.
- **Curious & Quick Learner:** Wants to understand new, complex products.
- **Resilient:** Can handle rejection and keep going.
- **Competitive:** Motivated to win, but works well with a team.
- **Professional:** Can confidently interact with senior executives.

### **5. Compensation & Benefits (What to Sell to Candidates)**
- **Total Target Earnings:** $280K–$350K per year (includes base salary and commission).
  - **Base Salary:** 50% of total (so $140K–$175K per year)
  - **Commission:** Uncapped (no limit to how much commission they can earn)
  - **Accelerators:** Extra commission for exceeding targets (double commission above 120% of quota)
- **Equity:** Stock options (ownership in the company)
- **Benefits:** 401(k) with company match, full health/dental/vision, unlimited paid time off, home office stipend, incentive trips for top performers.

---

### **6. Territory & Location**
- **Region:** Western US (California, Washington, Oregon, Arizona, Colorado, Nevada)
- **Remote:** Can work from home anywhere in this region.
- **Travel:** Must be able to travel within the region and to the San Francisco HQ once a month.

---

### **7. What to Avoid**
- **Transactional Salespeople:** Candidates who have only sold small deals or to small businesses.
- **No Enterprise Experience:** Candidates without experience selling to large, complex organizations.
- **No Tech Sales Background:** Candidates who have never sold software or technology solutions.
- **No Track Record:** Candidates who can’t show they’ve closed large deals or hit big targets.

---

### **8. How to Screen Candidates**
- **Ask About:**
  - The largest deal they’ve closed (amount, company size, sales cycle length)
  - How they find and win new customers
  - Experience selling to C-level executives (CIO, CTO, CISO)
  - How they manage long, complex sales processes
  - Any experience with cybersecurity or cloud products (if any)
  - Comfort with travel and remote work

---

### **9. Key Terms Explained (For Recruiter Reference)**
- **Enterprise Sales:** Selling to very large companies, usually with long and complex buying processes.
- **CISO:** Chief Information Security Officer (person in charge of security at a company).
- **ACV:** Annual Contract Value (the yearly value of a deal).
- **CRM:** Customer Relationship Management software (like Salesforce; used to track sales activity).
- **Proof of Concept (POC):** A trial period where the customer tests the product.
- **MEDDIC:** A structured approach for managing complex sales (not required, but nice to have).
