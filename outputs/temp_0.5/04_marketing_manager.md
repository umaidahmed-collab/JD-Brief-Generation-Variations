Absolutely! To create a truly comprehensive, recruiter-friendly brief, I’ll need to clarify several points with you first. These questions will help ensure that the brief is detailed, clear, and tailored for a non-technical, external recruiter who may not know your company or industry.

**Please answer the following questions:**

---

### 1. About the Company & Culture
- How would you describe GreenLeaf Organics’ company culture and work environment in simple terms?
- What is the size of the company (number of employees)?
- Are there any values or personality traits that are particularly important to your team (e.g., collaborative, fast-paced, mission-driven)?
- What is the management style of the immediate supervisor for this role?
- Why is this role open (new headcount, backfill, promotion, etc.)?

### 2. About the Team & Structure
- Who does this role report to (title and a brief description of that person’s role)?
- Can you provide a simple org chart or explain the marketing team structure?
- What are the roles and experience levels of the 4 direct reports?
- How does the marketing team interact with other teams (e.g., product, sales, leadership)?

### 3. Role Priorities & Success
- What are the top 3 priorities for this person in the first 6-12 months?
- What would success look like for this role after 1 year?
- Are there any specific projects or campaigns this person will immediately take over or launch?

### 4. Candidate Profile
- Are there any “must-have” experiences or skills you want to emphasize (e.g., specific product types, channels, or campaign types)?
- Are there any “nice-to-have” experiences that would set candidates apart?
- What types of companies or backgrounds have your best marketers come from?
- Are there any industries or backgrounds you want to avoid?

### 5. Technical & Marketing Skills
- Which marketing channels are the most important for your business (e.g., social, email, retail, influencer)?
- Which marketing tools or platforms are most critical (Google Analytics, HubSpot, etc.)? Are you open to candidates who have used similar tools?
- How important is hands-on digital marketing experience vs. team leadership?

### 6. Management & Leadership
- What is your philosophy on leadership? What management style works best in your company?
- Are you looking for someone who is more strategic or more hands-on (or both)?
- How much autonomy will this person have in making decisions?

### 7. Logistics
- Is relocation or remote work an option for exceptional candidates?
- What is your timeline for hiring and onboarding?
- Are you open to sponsorship or do candidates need work authorization in the US?
- What is the interview process (number of stages, types of interviews, assessments, etc.)?

### 8. Compensation & Benefits
- Is the salary range flexible for outstanding candidates?
- Is the bonus tied to individual, team, or company performance?
- How is equity structured (stock options, RSUs, etc.)?
- Any unique perks or benefits not mentioned in the JD?

### 9. Screening & Red Flags
- What are the biggest red flags or dealbreakers for you in candidates?
- Are there any companies or backgrounds you do *not* want to see?
- What are some common reasons candidates have failed in similar roles at your company or others?

### 10. Additional Context
- Is there any other context about the role, team, or company that would help a recruiter “sell” this opportunity?
- Are there any recent press releases, news, or achievements you want the recruiter to share with candidates?

**Once you answer these questions, I’ll create a clear, comprehensive, and jargon-free recruiter brief for you.**
