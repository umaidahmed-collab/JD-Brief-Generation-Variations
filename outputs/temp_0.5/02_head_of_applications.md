To create a truly comprehensive, easy-to-understand recruiter brief, I’ll need to clarify a number of details about your company, the role, your expectations, and any context that would help a non-technical, external recruiter succeed. Here’s a list of questions to help me gather all the information needed for a clear, jargon-free brief:

---

## 1. **Company & Context**
- **a. Company Overview:**  
  What does your company do? What is its industry, size, and main business focus?
- **b. Location:**  
  Where is the role based? Is it onsite, hybrid, or remote? Are there any relocation or work permit considerations?
- **c. Reporting Structure:**  
  Who does this role report to? Who reports into this role?
- **d. Team Size:**  
  How many people will this person manage directly and indirectly?
- **e. Key Stakeholders:**  
  Who are the main people or groups this person will interact with (e.g., government departments, business leaders, IT teams)?

## 2. **Role Purpose & Impact**
- **a. Why is this role important?**  
  What are the main goals and priorities for this position?
- **b. What are the main challenges or opportunities facing this role?**
- **c. What would success look like in the first 12 months?**

## 3. **Technical Environment & Applications**
- **a. ERP System:**  
  Which ERP system(s) do you use (e.g., SAP S/4HANA, Oracle NetSuite, Microsoft Dynamics 365 F&O)?  
  Are there other critical enterprise applications (please specify)?
- **b. Cloud/On-Premise:**  
  Are your applications hosted on the cloud, on-premises, or a mix (hybrid)? Which cloud providers do you use (e.g., Azure, AWS)?
- **c. Service Management Tools:**  
  Which tools do you use for IT service management (e.g., ServiceNow, Jira Service Management, Zendesk)?
- **d. ITIL Framework:**  
  Are there specific ITIL processes or certifications you require? (e.g., Incident Management, Change Management)

## 4. **Candidate Profile**
- **a. Must-have Experience:**  
  Are there specific industries or company types you want candidates to come from (e.g., government, large enterprise, consulting)?
- **b. ERP Experience:**  
  Is hands-on experience with a particular ERP system required, or is experience with any Tier 1 ERP sufficient?
- **c. Leadership Experience:**  
  What is the minimum size and type of teams they should have managed?
- **d. Certifications:**  
  Are any certifications mandatory (e.g., ITIL v4)?  
  Are there any preferred certifications?
- **e. Education:**  
  Is a Master’s degree required or just preferred?
- **f. Soft Skills:**  
  What personal qualities are most important (e.g., communication, diplomacy, strategic thinking)?
- **g. Language Requirements:**  
  Is English proficiency sufficient, or is Arabic (or another language) required or preferred?

## 5. **Vendor & Stakeholder Management**
- **a. Vendor Types:**  
  What types of vendors will they manage (e.g., software vendors, consulting partners, outsourcing firms)?
- **b. Contract Negotiation:**  
  What level of experience do they need with contracts? (e.g., negotiating pricing, service levels, renewals)

## 6. **Compensation & Benefits**
- **a. Salary Range:**  
  What is the base salary range for this role?
- **b. Bonus/Benefits:**  
  Are there bonuses, stock options, allowances, or other benefits?
- **c. Relocation/Expat:**  
  Is there a relocation package or expat support?

## 7. **Recruitment Process**
- **a. Interview Steps:**  
  What is your interview process (stages, panel, assessments)?
- **b. Timelines:**  
  How soon do you need someone to start? Are there any critical dates?

## 8. **Other Considerations**
- **a. Diversity & Inclusion:**  
  Are there any specific DEI goals or requirements?
- **b. Red Flags:**  
  Are there any backgrounds or experiences you want to avoid?
- **c. Anything else you want the recruiter to know?**
