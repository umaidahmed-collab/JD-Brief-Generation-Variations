Absolutely! To create a highly effective, recruiter-friendly brief, I need to clarify a number of details so we can provide maximum context, avoid confusion, and enable your recruiter to be as successful as possible. Here are the questions I’ll need you to answer:

---

## COMPANY & CULTURE

1. **Company Overview:**  
   - What is the company’s mission and vision, in simple terms?
   - What are your main products or bestsellers?
   - What is the company culture like? (e.g., collaborative, fast-paced, entrepreneurial, values-driven, etc.)
   - Is there anything unique about GreenLeaf Organics that would excite candidates?

2. **Team Structure & Reporting:**
   - Who will this person report to (by title)?
   - Who are their peers? (e.g., other managers, heads of departments)
   - Who is on their team (titles/roles of direct reports)?
   - Are there dotted-line relationships with other teams (e.g., product, sales, operations)?

---

## ROLE DETAILS

3. **Reason for the Hire:**  
   - Is this a new position, or a replacement? If a replacement, why did the previous person leave?

4. **Immediate Priorities:**  
   - What are the top 3-5 projects or objectives the person must tackle in the first 6-12 months?
   - Any big launches, deadlines, or business goals tied to this role?

5. **Success Metrics:**  
   - How will you measure this person’s success in the first year? (e.g., sales growth, campaign performance, brand awareness, team development)

---

## CANDIDATE PROFILE

6. **Must-have Experience Clarification**  
   - For “CPG/FMCG” experience: Will you consider candidates from other industries if they have similar skills? Or strictly CPG/FMCG?
   - Are you open to candidates from large companies, small/startups, or both?
   - How important is direct experience with organic/natural/sustainable products?

7. **Management Experience:**  
   - What is your preferred style of team leadership (hands-on, coaching, delegating, etc.)?
   - Are you looking for someone who has managed teams of a certain size or structure?

8. **Technical/Marketing Skills:**  
   - Which digital marketing channels are most important for you? (e.g., social media, search, email, influencer, retail)
   - For analytics: Is knowledge of certain tools (Google Analytics, Tableau) required, or just comfort with data?
   - Which marketing automation platforms do you currently use, if any?

9. **Agency/Vendor Management:**  
   - How many agencies are involved, and what are their main functions?
   - What is the ideal experience with agencies? (e.g., leading pitches, managing contracts, evaluating performance)

10. **Budget Management:**  
    - Will the person have full control over the $2M+ budget? Need to report/justify spend to anyone?

11. **Influencer & Social Media:**  
    - What scale of influencer campaigns have you run? (micro, macro, celebrity influencers?)
    - Are there particular social platforms you want to grow most?

---

## LOGISTICS & PRACTICALITIES

12. **Location/Relocation:**  
    - Is relocation assistance offered, or are you only considering local/nearby candidates?
    - Is the “hybrid” schedule flexible (e.g., which days in office, any travel expected)?

13. **Work Authorization:**  
    - Will you sponsor visas, or must candidates have existing US work authorization?

14. **Compensation Details:**  
    - Is the bonus guaranteed or discretionary? How is it calculated?
    - Is equity a standard grant or negotiable?
    - Are benefits standard, or is there anything unique/especially attractive?

15. **Interview Process:**  
    - What are the main interview stages?
    - Key people involved in decision?
    - Any assessments, case studies, or presentations required?

---

## INTANGIBLES

16. **Best-fit Personality:**  
    - What soft skills or personality traits are most important? (e.g., entrepreneurial, data-driven, creative, calm under pressure, etc.)

17. **Red Flags:**  
    - What are signs that a candidate probably wouldn’t be a fit?
