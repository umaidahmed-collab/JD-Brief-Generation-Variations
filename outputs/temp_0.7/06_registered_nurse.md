## KEY RESPONSIBILITIES (explained simply)

- **Direct Patient Care:** Hands-on care for people who come into the ER, including children and adults with injuries, illnesses, or mental health emergencies.
- **Assessment & Care Planning:** Checking vital signs, asking about symptoms, and planning what care each patient needs.
- **Administering Medications & Treatments:** Giving medicines, starting IVs (small tubes in the vein for fluids/medications), drawing blood, and caring for special lines used for treatments.
- **Monitoring & Responding:** Watching patients for any changes, especially if they get worse, and taking quick action if needed.
- **Using Emergency Equipment:** Operating machines like heart monitors, ventilators (to help people breathe), and defibrillators (to restart the heart).
- **Triage:** Deciding how urgent each patient’s case is, using a standard system called the Emergency Severity Index (ESI).
- **Teamwork:** Working closely with doctors, specialists, and other nurses.
- **Documentation:** Recording all care and observations in the hospital’s electronic system (Epic EMR – an online medical record).
- **Patient & Family Education:** Explaining medical information, treatments, and what to do after leaving the hospital to patients and their families.
- **Emergency Response:** Taking part in hospital-wide emergencies, such as a patient’s heart stopping (“code blue”) or major accidents (“trauma activations”).
- **Precepting:** Training and guiding new nurses or nursing students.
