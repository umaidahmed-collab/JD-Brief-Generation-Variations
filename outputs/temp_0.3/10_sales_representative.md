# Recruiter Brief: Enterprise Sales Representative  
**Company:** CloudSecure Technologies  
**Location:** Remote within Western United States (CA, WA, OR, AZ, CO, NV)  
**Travel:** 30-40% travel for customer meetings and events; monthly travel to San Francisco HQ  
**Compensation:** $280,000 - $350,000 OTE (On-Target Earnings), 50% base salary, uncapped commission, equity, full benefits, and more (see below)

---

## 1. **About CloudSecure Technologies**

CloudSecure Technologies is a fast-growing cybersecurity company. We help large businesses protect their data and digital assets from hackers and cyber threats, especially as they move their operations to the cloud (internet-based computing). Our customers are big companies (500+), and we protect assets worth over $2 trillion. We are well-funded and growing quickly.

---

## 2. **Role Overview**

We are hiring an **Enterprise Sales Representative** to win new large business customers (mainly Fortune 1000 companies—these are the 1,000 largest companies in the US). This person will focus on selling our cybersecurity software and solutions to companies with more than 5,000 employees. The role is focused on new business (not managing existing accounts).

---

## 3. **Key Responsibilities (What the person will do)**

**A. New Business Development**
- Find and approach new large companies that could use our cybersecurity products.
- Build a list (pipeline) of potential customers that is 3-4 times the annual sales target.
- Have conversations with potential customers to understand their security needs.
- Show and explain our products to customers (product demos).
- Manage long and complex sales cycles (it can take 6-12 months to close a deal).

**B. Sales Execution**
- Meet or exceed sales targets ($1.5M-$2M in new sales per year).
- Lead contract negotiations and close deals ranging from $200K to $2M+ per year.
- Create and follow plans for how to win each target customer.
- Organize product trials or pilot programs (called “proof-of-concept”).
- Prepare business cases and proposals for executives, showing the value of our solution.

**C. Relationship Building**
- Build relationships with top executives, especially those responsible for security and technology (CISO = Chief Information Security Officer, CIO = Chief Information Officer, CTO = Chief Technology Officer).
- Work with different decision-makers in the customer company (security teams, IT, purchasing, etc.).
- Find and develop “champions” (internal supporters) within target companies.
- Work with our customer success team to get customer references.

**D. Collaboration**
- Work with our technical sales team (Sales Engineering) to address technical questions.
- Coordinate with Marketing for targeted campaigns.
- Partner with our outbound prospecting team (BDR = Business Development Representatives).
- Give feedback to our Product team about market needs.
- Attend industry events and conferences.

## 7. **How to Identify Good Candidates**

**Look for candidates who:**
- Have sold software or technology to very large companies (5,000+ employees).
- Have experience closing large, multi-year deals ($500K+ per year).
- Have sold to security or IT executives (CISO, CIO, CTO).
- Can explain complex products in simple terms.
- Are comfortable with long, complex sales cycles (6-12 months).
- Have a track record of meeting or exceeding sales targets.
- Are based in (or willing to relocate to) the Western US territory.
- Are comfortable with frequent travel.

**Red Flags:**
- Only have experience selling to small or mid-sized businesses.
- Have never sold technology or software.
- Lack experience with large, complex deals or multi-stakeholder sales.
- Are not comfortable with travel or remote work.

## 8. **Interview Tips for Recruiters**

- Ask how they build relationships with multiple decision-makers.
- Ask about their experience with long sales cycles and how they stay motivated.
- Ask how they learn and explain technical products to non-technical buyers.
